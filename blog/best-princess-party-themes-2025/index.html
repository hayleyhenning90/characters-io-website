Best Princess Party Themes 2025: Top Characters & Trends

Discover which princess characters are most requested this year and find the perfect theme for your celebration.

Princess parties remain the most popular birthday theme for young children, and the landscape of favorite characters continues to evolve. Based on thousands of bookings, here's our comprehensive guide to the most popular princess party themes for 2025—and how to choose the perfect one for your celebration.

Top 5 Most Requested Princesses in 2025

1. Elsa (Frozen)

Why she's #1: Elsa has maintained her crown since Frozen's release in 2013. "Let It Go" remains an anthem, and her message of self-acceptance resonates deeply.

  • Best for: Ages 3-8, winter/ice themes, kids who love magic
  • Party style: Ice palace decorations, snowflake crafts, blue/white color scheme
  • Peak seasons: Year-round popularity, but especially winter

Read our complete Frozen party guide

2. Moana

Why she's rising: Moana's adventurous spirit appeals to today's parents who want strong, independent role models. Perfect for outdoor and summer parties.

  • Best for: Ages 3-8, summer/beach parties, adventurous kids
  • Party style: Ocean themes, tropical decorations, blue/teal colors
  • Peak seasons: Spring through fall, especially beach parties

Read our complete Moana party guide

3. Mirabel (Encanto)

Why she's trending: Encanto's music ("We Don't Talk About Bruno") became a cultural phenomenon. Mirabel's message about being special without magical powers resonates deeply.

  • Best for: Ages 3-10, kids who love music and dancing
  • Party style: Colorful Colombian-inspired, butterflies, family themes
  • Peak seasons: Year-round

Read our complete Encanto party guide

4. Rapunzel (Tangled)

Why she's beloved: The floating lantern scene creates stunning party decor inspiration, and Rapunzel's creative, artistic nature appeals to many children.

  • Best for: Ages 3-8, creative kids, hair-focused activities
  • Party style: Purple/gold colors, lanterns, hair braiding
  • Peak seasons: Year-round, beautiful for evening parties

Read our complete Rapunzel party guide

5. Ariel (The Little Mermaid)

Why she endures: The live-action remake boosted interest, and ocean/mermaid themes are evergreen. Perfect for pool parties.

  • Best for: Ages 3-8, pool/beach parties, ocean lovers
  • Party style: Under the sea decorations, teal/purple colors
  • Peak seasons: Summer, pool party season

Rising Trends for 2025

Wish (Asha)

Disney's newest princess from "Wish" (2023) is gaining popularity. Her star-magic theme and purple color scheme create beautiful parties.

Multi-Princess Parties

Rather than choosing one princess, many families are booking 2-3 characters for "Royal Ball" or "Princess Tea Party" themes. This is especially popular for larger parties.

"Adventurous Princess" Theme

Combining characters like Moana, Rapunzel, and Mirabel who are known for bravery and independence rather than traditional "waiting for a prince" stories.

Retro/Classic Princess Revival

With new live-action remakes, classic princesses like Snow White, Cinderella, and Sleeping Beauty are seeing renewed interest.

How to Choose the Right Princess

Ask Your Child

This might seem obvious, but let your child pick! Watch for which princess they:

  • Watch most frequently
  • Dress up as
  • Talk about
  • Recognize immediately

Consider the Venue

  • Pool/beach party? Moana or Ariel
  • Backyard/outdoor? Any princess works, Moana is great for summer
  • Indoor venue? Elsa, Cinderella, Belle work beautifully
  • Evening party? Rapunzel's lantern theme is stunning

Consider the Age Group

  • Ages 2-4: Gentle princesses like Snow White, Aurora; also mascot characters like Bluey
  • Ages 4-6: Peak princess interest—Elsa, Moana, Rapunzel all excellent
  • Ages 6-8: More sophisticated themes—Belle, Mirabel, adventurous princesses
  • Ages 8+: Consider if they still want a princess or something different

Consider the Message

Different princesses embody different values:

  • Elsa: Self-acceptance, embracing who you are
  • Moana: Courage, following your calling
  • Belle: Intelligence, inner beauty, kindness
  • Mirabel: Being special without "powers," family
  • Rapunzel: Pursuing dreams, creativity

Multi-Princess Party Ideas

Can't choose just one? Multi-princess parties are increasingly popular.

Royal Ball Theme

2-4 princesses attend a "royal ball" at your party. Great for larger gatherings.

Princess Tea Party

Belle + Cinderella + Aurora for a classic tea party setting.

Adventure Princess Party

Moana + Rapunzel for adventurous, brave princess themes.

Frozen Duo

Elsa + Anna together create the complete Frozen experience.

Sister Princess Combo

If you have siblings, one can meet Elsa while one meets Anna, or each chooses their favorite.

Pro Tip: Book Early

Popular princesses (especially Elsa and Moana) book up quickly, especially for weekend parties. Book 3-4 weeks in advance for best availability.

2025 Party Trends Beyond Characters

  • Instagram-worthy setups: Elaborate balloon arches, themed backdrops
  • Sustainability: Eco-friendly decorations and favors
  • Experience over stuff: Character entertainment over piles of gifts
  • Small celebrations: Intimate parties with fewer guests, more quality
  • Home parties: Backyards and living rooms over expensive venues

Final Thoughts

The "best" princess is the one your child loves most. While Elsa continues to reign supreme in popularity, newer characters like Mirabel are rising fast, and classic princesses never truly go out of style. Consider your child's personality, the party venue, and the values you want to celebrate when making your choice.

No matter which princess you choose, a professional character appearance creates magical memories that last a lifetime.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which princess is best for 3-year-olds?

For very young children, gentle princesses like Snow White or Aurora work well. Elsa is also great because of high recognition from Frozen. Some 3-year-olds prefer mascot characters like Bluey who are less "adult-looking."

Can I book two princesses from different movies?

Absolutely! Mix-and-match princess parties are very popular. Cinderella and Belle, Elsa and Rapunzel—any combination works for a "Royal Ball" theme.

What if my child changes their mind about which princess they want?

Contact us as soon as possible! We can often accommodate character changes if done with enough notice. This is common with young children.
