Planning Character Visits at Camp

Group Size Considerations

  • Small groups (under 20): One character, intimate interaction
  • Medium groups (20-50): One character, structured activities
  • Large camps (50+): Multiple characters or rotating visits

Age Appropriateness

  • Ages 3-5: Princesses, gentle mascots, simple characters
  • Ages 6-8: Any characters, superheroes very popular
  • Ages 9-12: Superheroes, adventure characters, avoid "babyish" options

Scheduling Tips

  • Avoid peak heat hours for outdoor appearances
  • Plan around meals and rest times
  • Allow transition time between groups if rotating
  • Build in photo opportunity time
  • Consider indoor backup plan for weather

What Characters Can Do at Camp

  • Lead themed games and activities
  • Visit each cabin/group for meet-and-greet
  • Perform shows or storytelling
  • Lead singalongs
  • Participate in meals (sit with campers)
  • Award prizes or recognize achievements
  • Photo sessions with each group

Frequently Asked Questions

Can characters visit multiple groups?

Yes! For larger camps, we can structure visits where characters rotate through different groups or age levels. This works well for half-day or full-day bookings.

Do you offer summer-long packages?

Absolutely. If you want character visits throughout your summer program (weekly theme days, for example), we offer discounted package rates. Contact us for a custom quote.

What if it rains?

We can perform indoors as long as appropriate space is available. Discuss backup plans when booking. Characters can perform in gymnasiums, cafeterias, or other indoor spaces.
