The Complete Frozen Party Guide: Elsa & Anna Celebration Ideas

Create an Arendelle-worthy celebration with our comprehensive Frozen party guide. Everything from decorations to "Let It Go" singalongs.

Since 2013, Frozen has dominated the princess party scene. The story of Elsa and Anna resonates with children worldwide, and a Frozen-themed birthday party remains the most requested princess party theme year after year.

This guide covers everything you need to create an authentic Arendelle experience, including how to bring Elsa and Anna to life at your celebration.

Why Frozen Parties Remain #1

Even a decade after the original movie, Frozen continues to captivate children because:

  • Relatable sisterhood: The bond between Elsa and Anna resonates with kids
  • Empowering messages: "Let It Go" is an anthem of self-acceptance
  • Stunning visuals: Ice and snow themes create beautiful party aesthetics
  • Amazing music: Kids know every word to every song
  • Frozen 2 renewed interest: A whole new generation discovered Arendelle

Frozen Party Decorations

Transform your space into a winter wonderland with these decoration ideas:

Color Palette

Stick to Frozen's signature colors:

  • Ice blue (primary)
  • Silver/white (accents)
  • Purple (Anna's color)
  • Touches of teal

Essential Decorations

  • Snowflake garlands: Hang from ceiling, doorways, and walls
  • White and blue balloons: Create arches or bouquets
  • Tulle icicles: Hang strips of white tulle for an ice cave effect
  • LED fairy lights: Create sparkle throughout the space
  • Frozen character cutouts: Elsa, Anna, Olaf for photos
  • White tablecloths with snowflake confetti: Simple but effective

DIY Ice Castle Backdrop

Create a photo backdrop using:

  • White and blue streamers twisted together
  • Cardboard castle turrets painted silver
  • Hanging snowflakes at various heights
  • Battery-operated LED lights woven through

Budget Hack

Dollar stores stock plenty of blue, white, and silver party supplies. You don't need official Frozen merchandise—the color scheme creates the magic.

Frozen Games & Activities

"Let It Go" Singalong

This is non-negotiable at a Frozen party. Have the lyrics ready (though kids probably know them by heart) and let everyone belt it out. Even better when Elsa herself leads the singing!

Freeze Dance

Play Frozen soundtrack songs. When the music stops, everyone freezes—just like Elsa's ice magic! The last one moving sits out until the next round.

Olaf Building Station

Provide materials for kids to build their own Olaf:

  • White play-doh or Model Magic clay
  • Orange pipe cleaners (for carrots)
  • Black buttons or beads (eyes and buttons)
  • Small sticks (arms)

Snowball Toss

Use white pom-poms or cotton balls as "snowballs" and have kids toss them into decorated buckets. Award prizes for most successful throws.

Ice Rescue Game

Freeze small toys (like Anna figurines) in ice blocks. Give kids spray bottles with warm water and toy tools to "rescue" the characters—just like rescuing Anna's frozen heart!

Coronation Ceremony

Crown the birthday child as the newest member of Arendelle royalty. A professional Elsa performer makes this moment extra special.

Frozen Party Food Ideas

Snacks

  • "Olaf Noses": Baby carrots in cups
  • Snowball cake pops: White cake pops with sparkle sprinkles
  • Frozen hearts: Heart-shaped sandwiches or cookies
  • Sven's carrots and dip: Veggie tray with ranch
  • Ice gems: Blue rock candy
  • Snowflake pretzels: White chocolate-dipped pretzels

Drinks

  • "Melted Olaf": Lemonade with a paper Olaf face on the cup
  • "Elsa's Magic Punch": Blue lemonade or Kool-Aid
  • Hot cocoa bar: Especially great for winter parties

The Frozen Cake

Options range from simple to spectacular:

  • Simple: Blue frosted cake with Elsa/Anna cake topper
  • DIY impressive: White cake with rock candy "ice" crystals
  • Bakery custom: Elsa's ice castle or character faces
  • Cupcake tower: Blue and white cupcakes with snowflake toppers

Hiring Elsa & Anna Characters

The ultimate Frozen party experience includes a visit from the Snow Queen herself. Here's what to know:

Elsa Solo vs. Elsa and Anna Together

Elsa alone is perfect for smaller parties or tighter budgets. She's the star attraction and provides plenty of magic.

Elsa and Anna together is ideal for larger parties (15+ kids) and creates the full Frozen experience. The sisters interact with each other and guests, doubling the entertainment value.

What Professional Frozen Characters Do

  • Grand entrance as the Snow Queen/Princess of Arendelle
  • Lead "Let It Go" and other Frozen singalongs
  • Perform coronation ceremony for birthday child
  • Face painting with Frozen designs
  • Lead games and activities
  • Photo opportunities with all guests
  • Tell stories from Arendelle

Booking Tips

  • Book early: Elsa is our most requested character—book 3-4 weeks ahead
  • Request specific songs: Let performers know favorites
  • Consider the season: Winter Frozen parties feel extra magical

Frozen Party Favors

Send guests home with Arendelle treasures:

  • Snowflake wands: Sparkly wands like Elsa's magic
  • Blue slime: "Elsa's ice slime" in small containers
  • Frozen stickers and tattoos: Always a hit
  • Small Frozen figurines: Dollar store finds work great
  • Lip gloss: In blue or purple colors
  • Snowflake cookies: In cellophane bags with ribbon
  • Hot cocoa packets: "Warm hugs from Olaf"

Package in white or blue bags with snowflake stickers and an "Thank you for celebrating in Arendelle!" tag.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Frozen still popular for birthday parties?

Frozen remains the #1 requested princess party theme. Elsa is consistently our most booked character, with popularity renewed by Frozen 2 and ongoing streaming availability.

Should I book Elsa alone or Elsa and Anna together?

Elsa alone works great for parties under 15 kids. For larger parties or the full Frozen experience, booking both sisters doubles the entertainment and creates authentic character interactions.

What songs should Elsa sing at the party?

Most Elsa performers know "Let It Go," "Into the Unknown," "Show Yourself," and other Frozen favorites. Let us know your child's favorites when booking!
