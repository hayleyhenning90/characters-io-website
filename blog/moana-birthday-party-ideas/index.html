Moana Birthday Party Ideas: Ocean Adventure Celebration Guide

Create a Motunui-worthy celebration with tropical decorations, island-inspired food, and the wayfinder herself.

In This Article

  • Why Kids Love Moana
  • Tropical Decorations
  • Ocean Activities
  • Island Food Ideas
  • Hiring Moana

Moana stands out among Disney princesses as a brave, independent wayfinder who saved her people. Her story resonates with children who love adventure, ocean themes, and empowering messages.

This guide covers everything you need to create an island paradise party, including bringing Moana herself to your celebration.

Why Kids Love Moana Parties

  • Adventure over romance: Moana's story is about self-discovery and bravery
  • Amazing music: "How Far I'll Go" rivals "Let It Go" in popularity
  • Outdoor friendly: Ocean/tropical themes work great for summer parties
  • Active celebration: Moana's adventurous spirit inspires active games
  • Inclusive: Boys often enjoy Moana parties too

Moana Decorations: Tropical Paradise

Color Palette

  • Ocean blue and teal (primary)
  • Coral and pink (tropical flowers)
  • Sandy tan (beach elements)
  • Palm green (tropical leaves)

Essential Decorations

  • Tropical leaf garlands (real or artificial palm leaves)
  • Hibiscus flowers scattered on tables
  • Blue and teal balloons with ocean wave patterns
  • Fishing nets draped as backdrop
  • Seashells, starfish, and sand dollars
  • Tiki torches (flameless for safety)
  • Paper lanterns in ocean colors

DIY Ocean Backdrop

Create waves using blue plastic tablecloths layered and gathered. Add paper fish, sea turtles, and Moana cutouts for photos.

Perfect for Pool Parties

Moana's ocean theme makes this ideal for backyard pool parties. Add floating flowers and ocean-colored pool noodles for extra tropical vibes.

Moana Games & Activities

"How Far I'll Go" Singalong

The essential Moana party activity. Even better when led by Moana herself!

Wayfinding Training

Teach kids basic navigation concepts like Moana learned:

  • Finding direction by the sun and stars
  • Reading ocean waves
  • Simple map reading

Heart of Te Fiti Hunt

Hide a green "heart" (painted rock or gem) and have kids search for it to save Te Fiti. Create a treasure map with clues.

Ocean Relay Race

Teams race to fill buckets with water using sea shells or cups. First team to fill their bucket wins!

Coconut Bowling

Use coconuts (or coconut-painted balls) to knock down pins decorated like Kakamora.

Limbo with Island Music

A classic party game that fits the tropical theme perfectly.

Lei Making Station

Provide flowers (real or artificial) and string for kids to make their own Hawaiian leis.

Island-Inspired Food

Tropical Snacks

  • Tropical fruit kabobs: Pineapple, mango, strawberries
  • Coconut treats: Coconut macaroons or coconut-covered everything
  • "Ocean water" Jello cups: Blue jello with gummy fish
  • Kakamora coconuts: Decorate coconuts with faces
  • Starfish sandwiches: Use star cookie cutters
  • Fish crackers: Goldfish crackers in shell cups

Drinks

  • "Ocean water": Blue lemonade or Sprite with blue food coloring
  • Tropical punch: Pineapple juice, orange juice, and ginger ale
  • Coconut cups: Serve drinks in coconut-shaped cups with umbrellas

The Moana Cake

  • Simple: Blue ombre frosting with shell decorations
  • DIY: Layer cake with ocean waves and Moana topper
  • Custom: Moana on her boat with ocean scene
